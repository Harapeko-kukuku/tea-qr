Gyokuro

Gyokuro is a delicate, high-quality green tea.
When brewed carefully, it brings out deep umami and sweetness.
Below is a simple guide for the best cup.

■ Prepare the water

Gyokuro tastes best brewed at a low temperature.
Prepare water at about 40–50°C.
Tip: pour boiling water into a cup and wait about 1 minute to cool.

■ Tea leaves

Use about 3g per serving (around 1 teaspoon) in a teapot.

■ Pour the water

Pour the cooled water (about 30ml per serving).

■ Steep

Wait about 2 to 2.5 minutes.
The leaves will slowly release umami during this time.

■ How to pour

Pour out every last drop.

■ Second & third infusion

• 2nd: about 70°C / about 30 seconds
• 3rd: boiling water / 1 second
Each infusion has a different character.

■ Enjoy the leaves

After brewing, the leaves can be enjoyed as “ohitashi.”
Add a little soy sauce or ponzu and eat them as a small snack.

Enjoy your slow tea time.
