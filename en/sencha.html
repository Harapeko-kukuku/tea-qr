Sencha

999 Sencha is a delicate blend of high-quality tea leaves.
Brewing carefully brings out its natural sweetness and umami.
Here is the basic brewing method.

■ Prepare the water

Sencha is best brewed at a slightly lower temperature.
Prepare water at about 60–70°C.
Tip: pour boiling water into a cup and let it cool for about 1 minute.

■ Tea leaves

Use about 3g per serving (around 1 teaspoon).

■ Pour the water

Pour the cooled water into the teapot (about 30ml per serving).

■ Steep

Wait about 1 to 1.5 minutes.
During this time the leaves slowly release their umami.

■ Pouring

Pour every last drop evenly.

■ Second & third infusion

• 2nd: about 70°C / about 30 seconds
• 3rd: boiling water / 1 second
Each infusion offers a different taste.

■ Enjoy the leaves

After brewing, the leaves can also be eaten as “ohitashi”
with a little soy sauce or ponzu.

Enjoy a calm and relaxing tea moment.
